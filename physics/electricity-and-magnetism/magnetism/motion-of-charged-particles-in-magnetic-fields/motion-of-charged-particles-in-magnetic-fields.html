Motion of Charged Particles in Magnetic Fields

The magnetic force is perpendicular to velocity, so it changes direction without changing speed (in the ideal model).

Trajectory
Circular motion
If a charged particle’s velocity is perpendicular to B, the magnetic force acts as a centripetal force, producing uniform circular motion.
  • Magnetic force provides centripetal acceleration
  • Radius depends on speed, charge, mass, and B
  • Direction depends on sign of charge
  • Speed stays constant (ideal case)
Start nano-lesson Perpendicular force means curvature.
Trajectory
Helical motion
If velocity has a component parallel to B, that component is unaffected. The perpendicular component produces circular motion, so the result is a helix.
  • Split v into parallel and perpendicular components
  • Parallel component stays constant
  • Perpendicular component circles
  • Pitch depends on vparallel

Frequency
Cyclotron frequency
In the non-relativistic model, the angular frequency of circular motion depends on the charge-to-mass ratio and the magnetic field strength.
  • Depends on q/m and B
  • Independent of speed (non-relativistic)
  • Connects to cyclotrons (conceptual)
  • Useful for identifying particles in fields

Device
Velocity selectors (conceptual)
With crossed electric and magnetic fields, you can arrange for electric and magnetic forces to cancel only for particles of a particular speed.
  • Force balance idea: E and B effects oppose
  • Only one speed passes straight through
  • Used in measurement devices (conceptual)
  • Emphasizes vector direction reasoning
