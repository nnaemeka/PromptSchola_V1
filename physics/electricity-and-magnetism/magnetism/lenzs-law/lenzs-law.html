Lenz’s law sets the direction: induction resists change.

Determine the direction of induced current by tracking how the induced magnetic effect opposes the flux change—an expression of energy conservation.

This topic

Lenz’s Law

Lenz’s law is a direction rule with a physical reason: without it, induction would create energy from nothing.

Direction
Direction of induced current
The induced current produces a magnetic effect that opposes the change in magnetic flux that caused it. This is not “opposing the field,” but opposing the change.
  • Identify whether flux is increasing or decreasing
  • Decide what induced B would oppose that change
  • Use right-hand rule to get current direction
  • Works for moving magnets or moving loops

Meaning
Energy conservation interpretation
Lenz’s law ensures energy conservation: inducing a current typically requires mechanical work or an energy source, because the induced effects resist the motion or change.
  • Induced currents create opposing forces/torques
  • Work input becomes electrical/thermal energy
  • Explains why generators require torque
  • Connects to “magnetic braking” intuition

Pitfalls
Common sign-convention errors
Most mistakes come from mixing up the loop’s chosen normal direction, confusing “opposes B” with “opposes flux change,” or flipping the right-hand rule mid-solution.
  • Define a surface normal and stick with it
  • Track sign of flux consistently
  • Separate “cause” from “response” steps
  • Be explicit about current direction conventions
