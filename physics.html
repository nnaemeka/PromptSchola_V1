Introduction: What is Physics?

Before diving into equations and problems, get a big-picture view of what Physics is and how it developed over time.

The Physics track starts with a gentle but serious introduction: What is Physics? You’ll see Physics as the study of matter, motion, energy, and the laws governing the universe — not just a list of formulas.

  • • Meet historical figures like Archimedes, Galileo, Newton, Faraday, Maxwell, and Einstein.
  • • See how ideas evolved from simple observations to powerful mathematical laws.
  • • Understand how modern fields (quantum, relativity, nuclear, particle physics) grow from the same core ideas.

This “Chapter 0” sets the tone and motivation. After it, you’ll be ready to explore Mechanics and the other major branches with more context and curiosity.

The lesson introduces Physics using the same 6-step structure:

  • • Orient: What Physics asks and why it matters.
  • • Understand: Big ideas like forces, fields, energy, and symmetry.
  • • Apply: Everyday examples that reveal hidden Physics.
  • • Work Through It: Guided prompts to connect history and concepts.
  • • Review & Practice: Key ideas and quick questions.
  • • Simulation Lab: Simple visualizations to see how physical quantities change.

From there, you can jump into Mechanics or any other branch, knowing how Physics fits together as a whole.
